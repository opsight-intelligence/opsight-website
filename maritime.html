The Thesis

Public markets react to earnings, news, and filings. Alternative data can observe physical production activity — hulls at the quay, docks occupied, vessels on sea trials — weeks before those events become visible through traditional reporting channels.

What We Track

Every signal is a factual measurement, traceable to its source

◉

Dock Occupancy (SAR)

Calibrated Sentinel-1 radar backscatter over each yard, turned into an occupancy proxy per scene — independent of cloud cover or daylight.

◎

Sea-Trial Events (AIS)

Derived vessel-movement events around yard waters. A new hull running speed trials is a strong, early signal of imminent delivery.

◐

Filings & Contracts (OSINT)

Public DART disclosures, procurement notices, and shipbuilding press — contract wins, counterparties, and delivery windows, structured.

◇

Yard Activity Index

A simple, explainable activity score per yard over time — read the timeline and tell a busy quarter from a quiet one at a glance.

▣

Delivery Cadence

Occupancy and trial trends projected into an expected delivery rhythm — a backlog-execution proxy built on observed, not reported, activity.

◈

Cross-Source Corroboration

When radar, AIS, and filings agree, confidence rises; when they disagree, the contradiction is flagged rather than hidden.
