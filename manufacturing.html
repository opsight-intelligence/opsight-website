Run Rate · Die Casting

DCM-401 underperforms — elevated MTTR and poor MTBF

Severity: High Confidence: 85% Correlational

Cycle-time dashboards scored this die ~99%. The downtime lens found the truth: MTBF of 15 min and MTTR of 19 min drove a Red status — 516 stops caused 121 hours of downtime, capping utilization at 49%.

Impact: ~12–15% of annual output, ≈$2,100–$2,520/yr at $0.50 margin/part

→ Investigate tooling wear causing mid-shift abnormal cycles
→ Verify material consistency across lots
→ Design review for frequent stops

Capacity · Injection Molding

PRESS-201 capacity loss is downtime-dominated

Severity: Critical Confidence: 85% Correlational

PRESS-201 reached only 28% of theoretical max output, with 99% of an 18,618-part loss attributed to downtime. OEE availability of 34% and MTBF of 11 min point to a systemic availability problem, not isolated events.

Target: lift availability to 50% of theoretical max (12,943 parts) within 14 days

→ Prioritize availability: analyze MTBF/MTTR patterns
→ Address recurring downtime events first

CT Efficiency · Stamping

Master-data misalignment, not performance, drives anomalies

Confidence: 85% Correlational

Sub-second cycle times, judged to the hundredth of a second. A fleet "102.8% efficiency" masks tool-level gaps: STAMP-503's 118% comes from an approved CT (1.40s) slower than actual (1.19s) — a standards error, not an operational one.

Fix: recalibrate approved CT to actual; re-baseline efficiency

→ Recalibrate STAMP-503 approved CT to 1.19s
→ Audit STAMP-502 approved CT against actual
→ Re-baseline after correction
